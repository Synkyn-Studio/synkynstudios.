Our Story

Our NASA Origins

Dr. Rawand Rasheed, our CEO and co-founder, developed the first version of Synkyn MICRA at NASA’s Johnson Space Center. The mission was to protect astronauts from fire and smoke aboard the Orion spacecraft on missions to Mars.

The challenge was removing toxic particles from cabin air in microgravity, where traditional filtration fails completely. The solution had to be compact, never clog, use minimal energy, and capture contaminants instantly.

That solution became Helix MICRA™. The Multiplexed Inertial Coalescence Refining Apparatus.

Now, with an exclusive worldwide license from NASA, we are applying that same technology to the buildings where people live, learn, work, and eat every day.

Never clogs

Continuous inertial coalescence keeps the airstream open — there is no filter media to load up and replace.

Microgravity‑proven

Engineered for the Orion cabin, where conventional filtration fails completely in zero g.

Minimal energy

Contaminants are captured passively, cutting the load — and the running cost — of building HVAC.

Instant capture

Particles are refined out of the air the moment they arrive, not gradually trapped over time.

Earth‑licensed

The Apparatus

A core engineered in orbit,
refined for Earth.

At the heart of every Synkyn MICRA unit sits a coalescence core: spinning geometry that draws particles out of the air by inertia alone. No membrane to clog, no element to swap — just a continuous, self‑clearing path for clean air.

Dr. Rawand Rasheed

Co-Founder & CEO

PhD, Mechanical Engineering - Rice University

NASA Johnson Space Center alum; invented Synkyn MICRA™ for Orion. Founded Synkyn Studios to commercialize that filtration for buildings.

Rawand spent over five years at NASA’s Johnson Space Center on thermal-fluid systems for spacecraft water recovery, carbon dioxide scrubbers, astronaut respirator filters, and other microgravity life support. He invented the patented Synkyn MICRA™ filtration system for Orion, designed to filter toxic air from fire byproducts on deep-space missions to Mars.

He founded Synkyn Studios to bring that technology to commercial buildings, cutting HVAC energy use while improving indoor health. The company has raised $19.3MM in venture funding and $2.3MM in non-dilutive support from agencies including NSF and DOE.

Rawand holds multiple patents and publications, including a top 5% journal by impact factor for Helix MICRA™. He has been named to Forbes 30 Under 30 in Energy & Green Tech, ACHR 40 Under 40, and the ASME Mechanical Engineering Magazine Watch List in 2025, and received Rice’s Herschel M. Rich Invention Award for Synkyn MICRA™.
